Sixx Authentics Grading Standards

Clear grading for trading cards, built for consistency, transparency, and trust.

Our grading system uses two parts: a Condition Grade for physical quality and a Market Context Score for rarity, originality, and collectability.

Overview

Two-part grading system

The numeric grade tells you how strong the card is physically. The market score tells you how special it is as a collectible.

Condition Grade

Measures physical quality only: front, back, corners, edges, visual appearance, autograph status, and provenance documentation.

  • Front and back condition
  • Corners and edges
  • Visual appearance
  • Autograph and provenance

Market Context Score

Measures collectability, scarcity, originality, significance, demand, provenance strength, and whether meaningful comps exist.

  • Scarcity and originality
  • Historical significance
  • Demand and liquidity
  • Comps availability

Condition Grade

Physical grading criteria

Each card is scored using the full front and back. Borderline cases should be handled conservatively and documented clearly.

Front condition 1.0 max

Damage, creases, stains, bends, writing, dents, tears, and scrapes.

Back condition 1.0 max

The same standards as the front, applied to the reverse side.

Corners 2.0 max

Sharpness, rounding, dings, whitening, fraying, and splits.

Edges 2.0 max

Chipping, whitening, rough cuts, fraying, and edge wear.

Visual appearance 2.0 max

Centering, alignment, registration, gloss, stock quality, and presentation.

Autograph 1.0 max

Authentic player autograph, clubhouse autograph, facsimile, or unverified signature.

Provenance 1.0 max

Documented ownership history, verified source, sealed origin, or chain of custody.

Scoring bands

How the grades read

These bands help customers understand the final result quickly and consistently.

Condition Grade Meaning
10.0 Pristine
9.0–9.9 Excellent
8.0–8.9 Strong with minor flaws
7.0–7.9 Clearly worn
Below 7.0 Significant wear or damage
Market Score Meaning

M1 Minor premium
M2 Moderate premium
M3 Strong premium
M4 Very strong premium

The first number shows physical condition. The M score shows market context, rarity, and collectability.

Grader note

If two possible grades are close, choose the lower one unless there is clear documented justification to move up. The goal is consistency, repeatability, and trust.
